About me

I graduated from Queen's University Belfast in 2021 with an MA in Public History awarded with distinction. Over the least five years I have worked on and lead numerous projects in Belfast and around Northern Ireland / the North of Ireland. Currently, I'm the Heritage Engagement Officer for the EastSide Partnership, a regenerative, place-based charity located in East Belfast, and I project manage the "Our Stories — Our Times" project for the British Red Cross. I am also the Oral History Society's Regional Networker for Northern Ireland.

I have previously worked for Queen's University Belfast, National Museums Northern Ireland, and the British Red Cross. And have further past experience with DfC Historic Environment Division, Armagh County Museum, Copius Group Consulting, and the Australian National University.

My work is especially place-based, and engages with contested histories or contentious topics. Methodologically, my work draws on microhistory, oral history, and the emerging field of vernacular history but my practise is participatory.

I work with communities to deliver projects, keeping participants at the core of those efforts.
